## General
AAPL is a dominant constituent of the consumer electronics and technology sectors, characterized by a deeply integrated ecosystem of hardware, software, and services. The instrument’s value is driven by the continuous lifecycle of its core hardware—primarily the iPhone—and the high-margin growth of its Services segment, including the App Store, Apple Pay, and iCloud.

The company's structural stability is closely tied to its global supply chain, particularly its manufacturing footprint in China and increasingly in India and Vietnam. Competitive dynamics are defined by its relationship with peers such as Microsoft, Alphabet, and Samsung, as well as its exposure to regulatory scrutiny regarding antitrust and digital market competition in the US and EU. Key structural factors include consumer discretionary spending trends, semiconductor availability, and geopolitical tensions affecting trade routes and manufacturing logistics.

## Stage: Article Processing
Articles are deemed relevant to AAPL if they contain specific signals regarding its hardware ecosystem, software services, or supply chain integrity. Critical keywords include "iPhone," "iOS," "Apple Silicon," "App Store antitrust," "Services revenue," "Vision Pro," and "supply chain" in the context of China or India. 

Decoy topics include general technology news regarding software-as-a-service (SaaS) that does not interact with the Apple ecosystem, or generic semiconductor industry updates that do not specifically impact Apple's custom silicon strategy. Articles discussing competitors (e.g., Samsung or Google) are relevant only if they directly threaten Apple’s market share or ecosystem lock-in. Articles regarding general macroeconomic indicators (e.g., inflation) are only relevant if they signal a shift in global consumer discretionary spending capacity.
